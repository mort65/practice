Complex Number Multiplication

A complex number can be expressed as (a, b) or as a + bi. a is called the number's real part, and b is called its imaginary part. i is defined by i x i = -1.

Write a program that reads complex numbers from the input, multiplies, and prints the result.

For example:

Input: 1, 2, 3, 4

Output: -5, 10

Explanation:

(1+2i) *(3+4i) = 1*3 + 1*4*i + 2*i*3 + 2*i*4*i = 3 + 10*i + 8*i*i = 3 + 10*i + -8 = -5 + 10i

Bonus: Implement complex number division.
